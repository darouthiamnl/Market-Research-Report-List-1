<p><p>The grooving tools market can be segmented into internal and external grooving tools. Internal grooving tools are designed for creating grooves inside pipes, tubes, or cavities, facilitating applications in precision manufacturing. In contrast, external grooving tools are used for machining grooves on the outer surfaces of workpieces, often utilized in industries like automotive and aerospace. Both types are essential for enhancing mechanical assembly, weight reduction, and improving the functional performance of components while catering to specific manufacturing needs.</p></p>
