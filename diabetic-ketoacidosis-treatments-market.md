<p><p>The Diabetic Ketoacidosis (DKA) treatments market includes three primary types: fluid replacement, electrolyte replacement, and insulin therapy. Fluid replacement is crucial for rehydrating patients and restoring blood volume. Electrolyte replacement addresses imbalances that may occur due to dehydration and metabolic disturbances, particularly with potassium and sodium levels. Insulin therapy is vital for reducing blood glucose levels and halting the production of ketones. Together, these treatments stabilize patients and prevent complications associated with DKA.</p></p>
