<p><p>The thrombosis drug market encompasses various types of medications used to prevent and treat blood clots. P2Y12 platelet inhibitors, such as clopidogrel, inhibit platelet activation, reducing clot formation. Xa inhibitors, like rivaroxaban and apixaban, target factor Xa in the coagulation cascade, preventing thrombin production and thrombosis. Heparin, an anticoagulant, enhances the activity of antithrombin III, leading to the inactivation of thrombin and factor Xa, effectively preventing clot formation. Each class addresses different mechanisms in thrombus management.</p></p>
